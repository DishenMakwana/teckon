## 7. Key Observations for Teckon Website

### 7.1 What JCB Does Well
- **Structured navigation** — 3-level deep menus with clear taxonomy
- **Industry-first filtering** — categorizes machines by use case (Agriculture, Mining etc.), not just product type
- **Parts taxonomy** — deeply categorized parts pages create SEO content opportunities
- **Blog content** — educational, non-salesy articles that build trust
- **News section** — keeps the site fresh and shows company activity
- **Quote/Brochure CTA** — two distinct calls to action at every product level
- **LiveLink upsell** — telematics feature integrated as an added value service

### 7.2 What Teckon Can Adopt
| Feature | Implementation |
|---------|---------------|
| Industry-based filtering | Filter parts by machine type and industry |
| Maintenance-focused blogs | Write educational content to rank on Google |
| Parts taxonomy navigation | Structured category pages (Filters > Engine Filters > JCB) |
| News/updates section | Supplier tie-ups, new stock, industry updates |
| PDF brochure / spec sheet download | For each product category |
| "Request a Quote" per product | Instead of generic contact form |
| Trust signals | "Genuine parts", "OEM quality", "Warranty" badges |
| WhatsApp CTA | Relevant for India market |

### 7.3 Content Strategy Gaps (What JCB Doesn't Cover Well – Opportunity for Teckon)
- **Aftermarket parts comparison** — JCB only sells OEM. Teckon can offer OEM vs aftermarket analysis
- **Price transparency** — JCB doesn't list prices; Teckon can differentiate with pricing clarity
- **Cross-brand compatibility charts** — JCB vs Hitachi vs Komatsu parts interoperability
- **Faster delivery messaging** — "Same-day dispatch", "Pan-India shipping" etc.
- **Used machine parts** — growing market JCB doesn't directly serve online
